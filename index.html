Rules

20 x 20 sea map with independent connected islands. Army units move 1 square, tanks and transporter ships move 2, and fighter planes move 6.

Both empires begin with 3 armies and 1 armored tank. Cities can produce armies, armored tanks, transporter ships, and fighter planes.

Armies have 5 HP and strike for 1. Tanks have 10 HP and strike for 2. Transporter ships have 3 HP and strike for 1. Fighter planes have 1 HP, strike for 3, and have fuel for 15 movements.

Fighter planes cannot capture cities. Fighter fuel counts squares flown: 15 fuel means 15 squares. A fighter refuels by returning to a friendly city; if its fuel reaches 0 away from a friendly city, it crashes.

Land units stay on land. Transporter ships move only on water. Fighter planes can fly over land and sea.

Armies and tanks can board a friendly transporter by moving onto the ship square. Units inside are locked and cannot move until the transporter unloads them.

A transporter carries up to 6 armies or 3 tanks, moves only on water, and can unload one carried unit to adjacent land when it is next to land.

10 cities produce units for their owner: armies take 3 turns, tanks 6 turns, transporter ships 5 turns, and fighter planes 6 turns. A city changes owner when a unit enters it.

Click a blue city to choose whether it will produce an army, armored tank, fighter plane, or transporter ship.

Enemy units on the same square fight randomly until one unit reaches 0 hitpoints.

Win when the opponent has no units, no owned cities, and every enemy transporter is sunk or neutralized so it cannot move or unload. Black unoccupied cities do not need to be conquered.
